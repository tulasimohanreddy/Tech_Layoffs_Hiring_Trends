# 📊 Tech Layoffs & Hiring Trends Analysis (2024–2026)

## 📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of Tech Layoffs & Hiring Trends from **2024 to 2026** using Python. The dataset was sourced from a real-world tech industry workforce dataset and analyzed to uncover workforce trends, hiring patterns, layoff factors, and the impact of AI automation across the technology sector.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze company-wise and industry-wise layoff trends.
* Identify major reasons behind workforce reductions.
* Examine hiring patterns and recruitment strategies.
* Explore AI automation and job replacement risks.
* Generate actionable business insights and recommendations.

## 📈 Key Insights

* 🔹 **OpenAI** recorded the highest layoffs (**983**), significantly above all other companies.
* 🔹 **Social Media** was the most affected industry, with **2,158 layoffs**.
* 🔹 **Overhiring Correction** was the leading reason for layoffs, accounting for approximately **25%** of all cases.
* 🔹 **ML Engineer** and **Data Scientist** emerged as the most in-demand roles despite ongoing layoffs.
* 🔹 Around **43% of companies implemented a Hiring Freeze**, reflecting cautious recruitment strategies.
* 🔹 The **United Kingdom (UK)** recorded the highest number of layoffs globally (**2,348**).

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Invest in high-demand skill areas such as Machine Learning, Data Science, and AI.
* Improve workforce planning to avoid overhiring and subsequent layoffs.
* Develop employee reskilling and upskilling programs.
* Monitor AI adoption carefully while balancing workforce retention.
* Focus on strategic hiring rather than aggressive expansion.
